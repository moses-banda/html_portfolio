About Me

Hi, I’m Moses — a tech enthusiast, community builder, and lifelong learner with a passion for solving real-world problems through innovation. Whether it’s developing a tactile algebra board to support blind students in Zambia or building an AI-powered waste management system at a global hackathon, I’m constantly exploring the intersection of creativity and technology. My journey began with curiosity and has grown into a deep commitment to designing tools that empower others.

I’m currently studying Electrical and Computer Engineering at Vanderbilt University, where I serve as a board member on the Student Alumni Board and contribute to campus initiatives through the Partnership Committee. From organizing alumni events and distributing tech tour tickets to helping students feel connected, I love finding ways to bridge generations through shared experiences. Outside of academics, I’ve developed public speaking skills through EducationUSA and enjoy helping others navigate their academic and career journeys.

When I’m not coding, leading events, or geeking out over chess (yes, I even have a birthday cake shaped like a chessboard!), you’ll probably find me exploring new ideas, playing a match online, or dreaming up my next big project. I believe tech should be inclusive, purposeful, and a little bit fun — just like me.
